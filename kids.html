World Animals

1. The African Elephant

Life Span: 60-70 years

Description: The African Elephant is the largest animal living on land today! They are famous for their giant ears, which help them keep cool in the hot African sun, and their amazing long trunks. A trunk is actually a very long nose that they use to breathe, smell, drink, and grab things—even tiny items! They have thick, grey skin and long tusks made of ivory.

Interesting Facts: Elephants are super smart and have great memories. They live in big family groups called herds, led by the oldest female elephant (the matriarch). They talk to each other using special sounds that are too low for humans to hear! They love to bathe in mud to protect their skin from bugs and the sun. 2. The Bengal Tiger

Life Span: 10-15 years

Description: Tigers are the biggest cats in the world! They are known for their beautiful orange fur with black stripes. Did you know no two tigers have the exact same stripes? It's like their fingerprint. They are incredibly strong swimmers and love water, unlike most other cats. They have powerful bodies and sharp claws to help them hunt.

Fun Facts: Tigers are lone rangers; they like to live alone. A tiger's roar is so loud it can be heard from nearly 2 miles away! Sadly, they are Endangered, which means they need our help to be protected. (Placeholder for 500+ words...)

World Trees

1. The Mighty Oak Tree

Life Span: Hundreds of years!

The Oak is a symbol of strength. They grow very big with strong trunks and produce tiny nuts called acorns. One big oak tree can give a home to hundreds of different birds and bugs!

Oceans & Aquatic Animals

The Big Blue Ocean

Oceans cover most of our planet! They are full of salt water and are incredibly deep. The ocean is home to the biggest animals (Blue Whales) and tiny coral reefs that look like underwater cities.
